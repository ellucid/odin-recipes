Chocolate Birthday Cake

Description

A decadent chocolate cake; fit for a birdathday! And the best part? It's quite an easy task! As long as you've got a cheap hand mixer,

a coupe of measuring cups, and a bowl, you can easily create this. Additionally, pre-mix batches of the dry ingredients can stay for

up to three months if stored in air tight containers, making for easy DIY cake mixes ready for any special occasion.

A recipe by Kat Boytsova

Ingredients

Makes one 9" cake

  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil, plus more for pan
  • 1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 3/4 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp. baking sode
  • 1 1/2 tsp. kosher salt, divided
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 3/4 tsp. vanilla extract, divided
  • 4 oz. semisweet chocolate chips or coarsely chopped chocolate
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 4 oz cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar

Steps

  1. Place a rack in center of oven and preheat to 325Â°. Grease the bottom and sides of a 9" roundcake pan with vegetable oil. Using

    the bottom of the cake pan, outline a circle of parchment paper to cut and place it in the pan.

  2. Combine all the dry ingredients - 1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour 1 3/4 tsp. baking powder, 1/4 tsp. baking soda, 1 1/4 tsp.

    kosher salt - and 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar in a large bowl. Sift in 1/2 cup cocoa powder, and whisk to combine.

  3. Whisk 2 eggs, 3/4 cup vehetable oil, and 1 tsp.vanilla extract in a medium bowl for about 30 seconds. The mixture should be

    Smooth and glossy. With a spatula, mix the egg mixture into the dry ingredients until fully homogenous. It may appear thick

    and pasty.

  4. Over medium heat, warm up 1 cup milk in a small saucepan. It should not boil, but rather simmer. Whisk the milk into the batter

    until just combined. It should be lump-free, but don't overmix

  5. Transfer the batter to prepared pan. Tap the pan against the surface to eliminate bubbles. Bake cake for 40-50 minutes,

    (rotating half-way through), or until the cake-tester/tooth-pick clean when inserted into the center. Transfer to wire rack to cool

  6. While the cake is baking, make the frosting. Fill a meduim pot and bring it to a simmer over medium heat. In a medium heatproof

    bowl, combine 4 ox. chocolate chips and 1/2 cup heavy cream. Place the bowl over the saucepan, reducing the heat to low, and

    stir frequently until chocolate is melted and mixture is homogenous. Chill ganache until cool (~25 minutes). Alternatively, melt the

    chocolate and cream mix in the microwave with 10-second bursts.

  7. Combine 4 oz. room-temperature cream cheese, 1/4 cup powdered sugar, and remaining 3/4 tsp. vanilla extract and 1/4 tsp. kosher

    salt in a large bowl. With a electric mixer on medium-high speed, whisk the ingredients until no lumps remain and texture is smooth.

    Beat in cooled chocolate ganache on medium speed until frosting peaks just holds its shape (~1 min). Do not overmix, or frosting

    will become too stiff.

  8. Remove the cake from the pan and invert it onto a plate. Spread the ganache evenly over the cakes surface.
